Favorite Video Game Characters of all time

  1. Vergil from Devil May Cry


    2. Vergil is the older twin brother of Dante and one of the main antagonists of the Devil May Cry franchise. Following the death of their mother as children, Vergil and Dante went their separate ways, with Vergil rejecting his humanity and embracing his demonic heritage, in contrast with his younger brother's embrace of humanity and initial rejection of the demonic. Stoic and reserved, Vergil displays a willingness to do anything in his quest to obtain the power of his father, Sparda.

  2. Kiryu from Yakuza 0



    3. Kiryu is the main protagonist for the majority of the Yakuza video game series developed by Sega's Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio. He's a legendary figure within the Yakuza underworld, earning the title "the Dragon of Dojima" due to his formidable strength, unwavering resolve, and the massive dragon tattoo emblazoned on his back. Kiryu starts as a rising star in the Tojo Clan, one of the most powerful Yakuza organizations in Tokyo.

    Despite his Yakuza affiliation, he lives with a strong sense of justice and moral code with compassion towards others and even children. This behavior was cultivated through his early life in an orphanage, spending time and care with other orphans and respected adults. Over the years, he vigorously trained his own street fighting styles that overpowered many enemies and foes along his journey to keep tokyo in safe hands.

  3. The Black Panther from Marvel Rivals



    4. Black Panther, also known as T'Challa and titled 'King Of Wakanda', is a playable Duelist hero in the 6v6 third-person hero shooter Marvel Rivals. He brings his Wakandan technology and fighting skills to the game, focusing on high mobility and close-quarters combat to disrupt enemy backlines. T'Challa possesses enhanced strength, agility, senses, and durability after consuming the sacred heart-shaped herb grown in Wakanda, Africa. He is also a brilliant scientist, master strategist, and highly skilled martial artist. His suit is typically made of Vibranium, a Wakandan metal that can absorb kinetic energy and repel it.

    Created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, Black Panther first appeared in Fantastic Four #52 issue comic in 1966, making him the first African superhero in American comic books. Before Marvel Rivals, he gained global recognition due to the debut MCU (Marvel Cinematic Universe) movie of "The Black Panther" in 2018. T'Challa is not just a superhero; he is a king responsible for his people and a figure navigating the complexities of tradition and the modern world. He has been a member of the Avengers and other superhero teams throughout his life.
